Modules  
  A full list of available modules can be found in our Economics postgraduate module guide  
  Core modules    
 
   Applied Behavioural Economics 
  
  
  
    The module will cover a selection of topics in applied behavioural economics where a substantial literature has already developed. Possible areas include behavioural approaches to: labour economics; public economics; financial economics; development economics and macroeconomics.  
   
   
    
   
  
    While the module is a natural choice for those studying for the MSc in Behavioural Economics, the module is designed and intended as a free-standing option accessible to all MSc students in the School of Economics. 
   
   
    
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     illustrate how behavioural approaches to economics can inform a broad range of applied economic analysi  
     consider what is distinctive about behavioural approaches and how these can inform theory, evidence and policy in the selected spheres of application  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Development Microeconomics 
  
  
   The module employs tools of microeconomic analysis to address topics central to development issues in low-income countries. One part concentrates on theoretical issues concerning (a) rural organisation (or agrarian structures) and (b) intra-household distribution, while the second part focuses on empirical issues concerning household behaviour covering intra-household allocation, production, risk, migration and fertility.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    acquaint students with modern economic theories as applied to microeconomic issues of concern in developing countries  
    give students experience in the interpretation and evaluation of empirical evidence relevant for the conduct of public policy in developing countries  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Development Policy Analysis 
  
  
   The module addresses a wide range of policy issues in the economics of development, using the format of two hour workshops. The issues to be addressed include:  
   
    promoting technical change  
    trade policy reform  
    welfare impact of tax reform  
    education policies  
    allocation and impact of development aid and policy conditionality  
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     familiarise students with a range of techniques for development policy analysis  
     provide a review of policy and practice in many of the most important areas of economic policy in developing countries  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Economic Data Analysis 
  
  
   This module provides students with "hands on" training in the use, presentation and interpretation of economic data, including time series, cross-section and panel data.  
   This comprises:   
   
    an introduction to basic principles of economic data analysis  
    survey design and sampling  
    data management  
    descriptive statistics  
    elementary regression  
    hypothesis testing  
    introduction to panel data  
    introduction to dynamic modelling  
    non-stationarity in economic time series  
    spurious regression  
   
   The module will also include a series of practical classes using various econometrics software packages.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    provide students with training in the use, analysis, presentation and interpretation of different types of economic data  
    provide competence in the use of econometric data analysis software  
   
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Economic Growth in Theory and Practice 
  
  
  
    The module introduces alternative theories of the growth process relevant to both industrial and developing countries with empirical applications: 
   
   
    
     exogenous growth theory and empirical implications  
     endogeous growth theory I  
     endogenous growth theory II and applications; empirical implications  
     unified growth theory  
     growth empirics analytical framework. accounting methods. convergence  
     panel approaches to growth empirics   
     time series and panel time series approaches to growth empirics  
     understanding patterns of economic growth  
     student-led presentations – “fundamental causes of growth” (Part A)  
     student-led presentations – “finance and development” (Part B)  
    
   
     This 
    module aims to: 
    
    
    
     
      provide students with an up-to-date graduate-level introduction to the economics of growth theory, focusing in particular on cross-country comparisons, and on the role of technical change (TFP)  
     
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Economic Policy Analysis 
  
  
   This module covers a series of topics in economic policy. The first part looks at policies relating to growth, technology, investment and exporting/trade, while the second part covers international economic policies and institutions. This includes:  
   
    policy transfer  
    macroeconomic policy (IMF)  
    development (World Bank)  
    aid and capital flows  
   
   This module aims to:  
   
    familiarise students with alternative perspectives on the role of the state in a modern industrialised economy  
    encourage students to evaluate critically alternative perspectives on state activity  
    analyse specific problems arising in the analysis of public policy  
    introduce the role of international actors in economic policy

    a review of perspectives on the principles and philosophical foundations of economic enquiry  
    the construction and evaluation of theories and research programmes  
    the role of models and concepts of rationality in economics  
    alternative empirical methods  
    professional practice  
   
  
    This 
   module aims to:

     introduce alternative research methods and to provide a grounding for the student disseratations  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Economics of Corporate Finance 
  
  
   The module offers an introduction to the economics of Corporate Finance and covers a variety of topics, with substantial time devoted for covering issues directly related to the financial needs of firms, such as capital structure, credit rationing and corporate governance.   
   The module also examines the role of financial intermediaries analyzing bank failures and, consequently, the scope for banking regulations. The last part of the module looks closely at the relationship between the financial sector and the real economy thus offering the background for any applied work related to the link between financial development and economic fluctuations.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    provide a guide to this new area of financial economics  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Economics of Household Finance 
  
  
   The module covers the central issues in the economics of household finance. Household finance is the study of the behavior of individuals and households in financial markets including those for secured (e.g. mortgage) and unsecured (e.g. credit card) lending and related economic models of consumption smoothing, liquidity constraints and household behavior.  
   The module begins with the central topic of consumption smoothing, focusing on the role of credit markets and income risk in household behavior. Later topics include financial literacy, self-control, mortgage market design, stock market participation and the regulation of consumer credit markets.   
   This module aims to:  
   
    give students a sound knowledge of modern economics of household finance  
    introduce students to theories of consumer behavior in credit markets  
    help students understand the ways in which economic analysis can be used to formulate models of consumer financial decisions  
    show students how these models can be tested  
    show students how the outcomes of these tests contribute to the understanding of household behavior and policy implications

    This 
   module aims to:

   Macroeconomic Analysis 
  
  
   The module covers core issues in macroeconomics:  
   
    growth and business cycles  
    consumption and savings rational expectations  
    frictions in product and credit markets  
    inflation and monetary policy  
   
   This module aims to:  
   
    consider a number of key areas in macroeconomics which are important both for model-building and policy analysis  
    build an understanding of relevant analytical methods, both theory and model-based, and an appreciation of the inter-temporal aspects of modern macroeconomics  
    build an appreciation of some important contemporary developments in macroeconomics that are relevant for policy analysis

    theories and evidence on trade and development  
    instruments of trade intervention and regulation

  Optional modules    
 
   Advanced Macroeconomics 
  
  
   This module covers the theory for the solution and estimation of dynamic stochastic models that are widely used in all fields of macroeconomics. The module is structured in a way such that students will be exposed both to theory and the practical implementation of the methods taught.  
   It covers topics from approximation methods for stochastic non-linear macroeconomic models, such as linear and higher-order Taylor approximation as well as dynamic programming techniques. It also exposes students to the empirical evaluation of these models ranging from calibration to classical and Bayesian estimation methods.   
   The module applies the techniques to contemporary general equilibrium macroeconomic models designed for positive and policy analysis such as the New Keynesian model but also models that are designed to explain partial equilibrium behaviour such as consumer saving and industry investment.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    introduce the theory and estimation of dynamic stochastic macroeconomic models  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Applied Microeconometrics 
  
  
   The module examines econometric problems in modelling cross section and panel data. Topics include:  
   
    probit, logit, tobit and Heckman models  
    selectivity  
    duration data  
    markets in disequilibrium  
    modelling panel data  
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     provide students with sufficient background in modern microeconometrics to choose techniques suited both to the data-source and the economic model  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Advanced Microeconomic Theory 
  
  
   This module examines central theoretical aspects from modern microeconomic theory, paying particular emphasis on game theory, imperfect competition and incomplete information.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    provide students with a solid theoretical background in advanced microeconomic theory, especially auctions, theory of the firm and bargaining  
    provide students with the knowledge and tools that will allow them to carry out their own original research in this area  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Behavioural Economic Theory 
  
  
  
    The module explores the psychological underpinnings of economic behaviour and of recent theories in behavioural economics.  
   
   
    
   
  
    Topics covered include:  
   
   
   
    
     introduction  
     choice, risk and uncertainty  
     reference-dependent preferences and loss aversion  
     time, preference and choice  
     stability of preferences  
     measuring social preferences  
     models of social preferences  
     models of strategic thinking  
     learning in games  
    
   
     This 
    module aims to: 
    
    
    
     
      
       acquaint students with the central elements of behavioural economic theory and of the psychological concepts and evidence which motivate it  
       broaden students' knowledge of how psychological and experimental findings influence economic modelling, especially in relation to preferences and judgement in situations involving risk, time or interaction between agents  
       improve students' modelling skills and appreciation of how a psychological perspective may contribute to the understanding of economic phenomena  
      
     
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Financial and Macro Econometrics 
  
  
  
    The module extends the coverage of advanced econometric modelling techniques and considers their application through the study of selected topics in finance and macroeconomics, developing familiarity and critical awareness of empirical research in these areas.  
   
   
    
   
  
    It covers techniques for the analysis of stationary ARMA processes, Vector Autoregressions (VARs), linear regression models, linear systems of simultaneous equations, cointegration, long-run structural VARs, forecasting, and models of changing volatility. The selected topics include the econometric analysis of business cycle fluctuations, wage, price and (un)employment determination, portfolio choice and stock market returns. 
   
   
    
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     provide students with the analytical techniques necessary to be able to read and critically evaluate published academic literature involving econometric analysis in macroeconomics and finance  
     provide familiarity with the key features of the published academic literature in selected areas of macro and financial econometrics  
     offer an understanding of the research methods available to academic economists, together with the ability to apply these through project work  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Industrial Organisation 
  
  
   The module examines central theoretical aspects of industrial organisation.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    provide students with a solid theoretical background to allow them to follow the theoretical debate in the most relevant journals in the IO area, such as the RAND Journal of Economics, the Journal of Industrial Economics, the International Journal of Industrial Organisation and the Journal of Economics and Management Strategy  
    impart students with the knowledge and tools that will allow them to carry out their own original research in this area  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   International Macroeconomics 
  
  
   International linkages in economics as a result of exchange rate movements, capital movements and spillovers. Factors which determine the level of the exchange rate and trade effects, target zone models, analysis of the relationship with fundamentals, regime switches and stop-loss traders. Policy coordination.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    address the major developments in international macroeconomics  
    cover the theory of exchange rates and target zones, balance of payments and speculative attacks, and currency crises and contagion  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   International Trade Theory 
  
  
   The module provides an overview of the theory of international trade, the theory of trade policy and each of their applications, utilising the techniques of general equilibrium theory and the theories of perfectly competitive and imperfectly competitive markets as appropriate. Recent developments in these areas will be emphasised.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    offer an up-to-date overview of the theory of international trade, the theory of trade policy, and of each of their applications  
    provide an insight into recent developments and place them in the context of the intellectual development of trade theories  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Monetary Theory and Practice 
  
  
   The module covers monetary aspects of advanced macroeconomics and is suitable for students of mainstream economics, finance and international economics. It focuses on the theory and practice of central banking, monetary policy and control.  
   This module aims to:  
   
    address the major developments in macroeconomics with an emphasis on monetary economics  
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Public Economics 
  
  
   Topics covered in the module will include:  
   
    taxation  
    general principles and specific taxes (income, consumption, wealth)  
    political economy  
    procurement/regulation   
    expenditure (health/education/welfare)  
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     give the students the necessary background to follow the theoretical and empirical debate in the most relevant journals in the Public Finance area, such as the Journal of Public Economics and the Journal of Public Economic Theory  
     build an understanding of the theoretical principles underlying the organisation of the state, the principles of taxation and the provision of private goods, such as health and education  
    
   
  
 
     
  
 
   Time Series Econometrics 
  
  
   The module examines fundamental properties of time series and various classes of stochastic processes. It focuses on:  
   
    issues in estimation and forecasting of time series models  
    concepts of contemporary interest to time series econometricians  
   
  
    This 
   module aims to: 
   
   
   
    
     provide students with a knowledge of the core techniques of econometric analysis   
     develop the analytical skills required to demonstrate theoretical asymptotic properties of different econometric estimation and testing procedures under weakened modelling assumptions  
     provide a proper understanding of the applicability and limitations of non-linear regression analysis
